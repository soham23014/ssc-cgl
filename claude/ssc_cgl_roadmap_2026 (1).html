⚡ How to use this roadmap as a working person
You have ~2.5 hrs on weekdays and ~10 hrs on weekends. That's ~22.5 hrs/week — more than enough if you're consistent and focused. This roadmap is designed around that reality.

Weekdays: Early morning (1 hr concept) + late night (1.5 hr practice). Lunch break = GA facts / vocab flashcards.
Weekends: Deep dives, PYQs, full mock tests and detailed error analysis.
📐 Quant 25 Qs
Profit/Loss, Geometry, Trig are the big 3 — 15–17 Qs from these alone
🧩 Reasoning 25 Qs
Analogy, Series, Coding-Decoding = highest yield, fastest to improve
📖 English 25 Qs
Error spotting + Vocab (Synonyms, Idioms) = 12–14 marks guaranateed
🌍 GA/GK 25 Qs
History, Science, Polity = static + fast to score. Revise from Lucent's
⚠️ Negative Marking — Critical Rule
Tier 1 has –0.5 marks per wrong answer. Never guess blindly. If you can eliminate 2 options → attempt. Else skip. Target: attempt 85–90 Qs with 90%+ accuracy rather than 100 Qs with guesses.
💡 Working professional mantra: 2.5 hrs/day on weekdays + 5 hrs each weekend day = exam cracked. Your edge over full-time students is discipline and focus per hour. No wasted time. Use commute for vocab + GA flashcards (Testbook app).

With limited time, focus on: Reasoning (fastest ROI) → GA static GK (Lucent's once = done) → English vocab + grammar → Quant top 3 chapters

🎯 Smart PYQ strategy for a working person
1. Don't do full papers first — solve topic-wise PYQs by chapter (e.g., all Profit/Loss Qs from 2019–2024 in one go)
2. Kiran's Chapterwise PYQ book is your bible — 8000+ Qs sorted by topic. Buy this first.
3. After Week 8, switch to full timed papers (shift-wise from Testbook / Oliveboard)
4. Maintain an error log notebook — note wrong Qs + reason → review every Sunday
5. Focus on last 3 years most (2021–2024) — SSC pattern repeats heavily in recent years
6. Year-wise PYQ papers are available free on SSC official website (ssc.gov.in) or download from Testbook
📋 What to note in your error log
For every wrong question, write: Date | Subject | Topic | My answer | Correct answer | Why wrong (concept gap / calculation error / silly mistake)

Review this log every Sunday morning for 20 min. This single habit separates average scorers from toppers.

⚠️ Negative marking rule — memorize this
–0.5 marks per wrong answer. This is not a guess-fest.

✅ Attempt if: You can eliminate 2 options confidently → probability is in your favour
❌ Skip if: You have no idea even after reading → saves 0.5 marks

Target: Attempt 85–90 out of 100 questions with 90%+ accuracy. This gets you ~145–155 marks, which is cut-off+ for most categories.
