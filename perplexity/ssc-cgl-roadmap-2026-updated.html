What changed

The earlier version was attractive but methodologically weak for SSC CGL 2026. It delayed serious mocks, spread Reasoning too thin, gave too much space to low-return Quant chapters, used an unrealistic vocabulary load, and lacked a proper score-tracking and weak-topic system.

Mock timing fixed

  • Sectional mocks start in Week 2.
  • First full mock starts in Week 5.
  • From Week 10 onward: minimum 2 full mocks each week.
  • Last 6 weeks: 3–5 full mocks every week.

Priority order fixed

  • Reasoning first for fastest return on time.
  • English daily for stable scoring.
  • Quant focused on high-frequency chapters.
  • GA sequence: Science → History → Polity → Geography → CA → Economy.

Tracking added

  • Score tracker with phase targets.
  • Error log categories for every mock.
  • Weak-topic dashboard.

Phase 1 master plan

This upgraded Phase 1 is built to be complete enough that you should not need a separate explanation page for what to study, why to study it, how to practice it, and what score level to expect by the end of the first 3 weeks.

Phase 1 goal: Weeks 1–3

  • Build fast-scoring Reasoning strength from the very beginning.
  • Create Quant base through SSC-friendly arithmetic plus simplification and number system.
  • Make English measurable every single day instead of vague.
  • Finish static GK in a tight order: Science, then History, then Polity basics.
  • Start topic-wise PYQs from Day 1, not after theory ends.
  • End Week 3 with section-level score targets and sectional test exposure.

English daily system

Daily 30–40 minutes minimum

  • 10 vocabulary words, but revise old words on the same day.
  • 15 grammar questions focused on error spotting, fill in the blanks, and sentence correction.
  • 1 short reading passage to keep comprehension active.
  • 10 previous-year English questions daily.
  • Keep a tiny notebook of repeated grammar rules and repeated vocab traps.

GA weekly order

  • Week 1: Science — Physics, Chemistry, Biology basics and repeated factual areas.
  • Week 2: History — Ancient overview, Medieval selective, Modern more important.
  • Week 3: Polity basics — Articles, Fundamental Rights, DPSP, Parliament, President, PM, constitutional basics.
  • Do not try to finish all Lucent randomly. Read only what matches the week target and revise it with PYQs.

Step-by-step method for every topic

  1. Read concept from one source only.
  2. Solve 15–20 topic-wise SSC PYQs the same day.
  3. Write short notes: formula, pattern, common trap, shortcut.
  4. Mark wrong questions by reason: concept, speed, calculation, reading.
  5. Reattempt the same weak questions after 48 hours.
  6. At week end, solve one mixed practice set from all completed topics.

Topic-wise quick tricks

  • Analogy: first identify relation type — number, word meaning, function, class, part-whole.
  • Series: check difference, second difference, alternating pattern, multiplication, prime pattern.
  • Alphabet series: memorize A=1 to Z=26 and reverse positions; this alone saves time.
  • Blood relation: draw tree symbols, avoid mental chain solving.
  • Direction: always start with north-facing arrow and update only after turns.
  • Order ranking: use total = left + right - 1 formula in direct questions.
  • Simplification: fraction-to-percentage conversions and square tables make SSC arithmetic much faster.
  • Number system: divisibility rules and remainder patterns remove many long methods.
  • Percentage: memorize 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5 type conversions permanently.
  • Ratio: equalize ratios instead of using long algebra where possible.
  • Average: total = average × number of items should become automatic.
  • Time & Work: work with efficiency units, not raw time statements.

  • Friday night: no new topic.
  • Revise all formulas studied that week.
  • Revise all vocabulary and grammar rules.
  • Revisit weak questions and marked PYQs.
  • Review the error log for repeated mistakes.

Why this Phase 1 is better

  • It adds simplification and number system, which support many SSC Quant questions indirectly and directly.
  • It upgrades Reasoning with alphabet series and missing number series, both common SSC scoring areas.
  • It turns English into a measurable daily block instead of a vague reminder.
  • It improves GA order by inserting Polity basics in Week 3 for high return.
  • It starts PYQs from Day 1, which matches how serious SSC aspirants learn pattern recognition early.
  • It adds revision and score targets, so the roadmap can actually be checked against output.

PYQ and exam method

SSC rewards familiarity and execution. PYQs should not be an afterthought.

PYQ tracker

  • Start chapterwise PYQs early, not just full papers.
  • By mid-phase, shift to timed mixed papers.
  • Keep a reattempt list of previously wrong PYQs.
  • Prioritize recent years more heavily.

SSC 2026 trend logic

  • Questions are usually more direct than banking-style puzzle exams.
  • PYQ repetition and theme repetition remain important.
  • Speed matters more than showing very advanced difficulty handling.
  • Accuracy matters more than chasing 100 attempts blindly because of negative marking.

Final reminders

This plan is designed to be sustainable till the exam, not exciting for three days and abandoned after two weeks.

Do this

  • Review every mock properly.
  • Revise small notes daily.
  • Protect sleep in the final phase.
  • Keep Reasoning and English active throughout.

Do not do this

  • No 50-word daily vocab targets.
  • No random new books in the last phase.
  • No over-investment in low-yield Quant chapters early.
  • No newspaper-heavy current-affairs routine.

Target mindset

  • Attempt smart, not reckless.
  • Build 145+ capability gradually.
  • Use trackers every week.
  • Let mocks decide your next week’s priorities.
