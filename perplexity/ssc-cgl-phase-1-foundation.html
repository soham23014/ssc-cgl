Reasoning

Core topics and solving approach

These Reasoning topics were chosen because they repeat often and can become quick marks with the right method. The main target is to move toward 20+ correct by the end of Week 3.

Analogy High importance

Always identify the relation type first: meaning, function, part-whole, category, or numerical relation.

TrickFind one clean relation before checking options.
Common mistakeGuessing from similarity without knowing the exact relation.
Practice20 PYQs after concept completion.

Classification High importance

Look for the one hidden mismatch only. Most questions are simple when you avoid overthinking.

TrickCheck group, property, use, and sequence quickly.
Common mistakeTrying too many logic patterns at once.
PracticeOdd-one-out sets in timed mode.

Series High importance

SSC often rewards simple pattern recognition here, especially number and letter patterns.

TrickCheck difference, second difference, multiplication, and alternation in that order.
Common mistakeSkipping the easiest arithmetic pattern and jumping to complex logic.
Practice20–30 questions in one sitting.

Alphabet Series High importance

This is repetitive and highly scoreable if alphabet positions are automatic.

TrickMemorize A=1 to Z=26 and reverse positions too.
Common mistakeForgetting reverse alphabet checks.
PracticeFast drills, not long theory sessions.

Missing Number Series High importance

This gives easy marks when you build speed at spotting the pattern family.

TrickCheck difference, multiplication, square relation, and alternate chains.
Common mistakeWasting too much time on a single stubborn question.
PracticeTimed mini sets.

Coding-Decoding High importance

This is one of the most repeated SSC Reasoning topics and should become a strength.

TrickTest shift, reverse, direct mapping, and word pattern one by one.
Common mistakeMixing all possible methods together without structure.
Practice20 PYQs the same day.

Blood Relation Medium importance

Usually direct, but chain questions become risky without a diagram.

TrickDraw a quick family tree every time.
Common mistakeTrying to solve relation chains only in your head.
PracticeShort and long relation sets.

Direction Medium importance

Direction becomes easy when every turn is diagrammed instead of imagined loosely.

TrickUse arrows and right-left turns on paper.
Common mistakeConfusing left and right after multiple turns.
PracticeDiagram-heavy drills.

Order Ranking Medium importance

These questions reward clean formula use rather than intuition.

TrickUse total = left rank + right rank - 1 when required.
Common mistakeMixing position with final rank.
PracticeFormula-based repetition sets.
Quant

Core arithmetic for Phase 1

The Quant mix here is narrower than broad syllabus coverage on purpose. Simplification and Number System were added because they support fast solving across SSC arithmetic and make the rest of the section easier.

Simplification Very high

This is one of the fastest scoring areas and strengthens raw calculation speed.

TrickMaster BODMAS, fraction-decimal-percent conversion, and square tables.
Common mistakeSlow basics creating time pressure in easy questions.
PracticeDaily timed drills.

Number System Very high

Strong Number System improves divisibility, factor logic, remainders, and mental comfort across Quant.

TrickLearn divisibility rules, unit digits, LCM-HCF, factors, and remainder patterns.
Common mistakeUsing long arithmetic where pattern logic is enough.
PracticeShort PYQ clusters with concept tagging.

Percentage Very high

This topic supports Profit and Loss, SI-CI, Average, and data interpretation later.

TrickMemorize common fraction-percentage conversions.
Common mistakeSlow mental conversion.
PracticeRepeated short sets with oral calculation.

Ratio & Proportion High importance

Directly asked and also useful later in comparison and distribution questions.

TrickEqualize ratios cleanly and reduce unnecessary algebra.
Common mistakeMixing percentage and ratio base values.
PracticeChapterwise PYQs same day.

Average High importance

Simple once total-sum logic is internalized.

TrickThink total first, average second.
Common mistakeForgetting sum changes when values are added or removed.
Practice20 mixed average problems.

Profit & Loss High importance

A consistent SSC favorite and one of the most useful scoring chapters.

TrickKeep CP, SP, MP, discount, and profit base values separate.
Common mistakeApplying percent on the wrong base.
PracticeConcept plus same-day PYQs.

SI / CI Medium importance

Usually formula-friendly and not hard if the basics are clean.

TrickWrite principal, rate, and time before substitution.
Common mistakeSubstituting quickly without reading the interest condition properly.
PracticeSmall focused sets.

Time & Work Medium importance

This chapter gets easier when you switch from raw time to efficiency thinking.

TrickUse LCM and work efficiency method.
Common mistakeSolving everything with direct fractions and getting stuck.
PracticePattern-based sets with worker combinations.

Mocks and revision

Testing, review, and weekly reset

Sectional mocks are not just for checking marks. They show whether your method is working, whether your speed is usable, and which mistakes keep repeating.

Mock plan

  • Week 1: no formal mock, only topic drills and PYQs.
  • Week 2: 2 sectional mocks minimum.
  • Week 3: 4 sectional mocks — Quant, Reasoning, English, Mixed.
  • After each mock, write errors in an error log the same day.

Friday night revision rule

  • Revise formulas.
  • Revise vocabulary.
  • Revise weak questions.
  • Review error log.
  • No new topics on Friday night.

Daily routine

Simple study flow to repeat every day

Keep the order fixed so your effort stays consistent and your revision never gets skipped. The structure matters as much as the topics.

Daily order

  • One concept block
  • 15–20 PYQs immediately after
  • Short note making
  • English daily block
  • Small GA block
  • End-of-day revision

Avoid these mistakes

  • Do not postpone PYQs for later weeks.
  • Do not learn 50 new words per day and forget them.
  • Do not keep adding new topics without revision.
  • Do not judge progress only by hours studied.
